### Markets and Food Areas

#### Sunday Walking Street (ถนนวัวลาย, Wualai Walking Street)
- **Hours**: Sunday evenings only (typically 17:00–22:00)
- **Type**: Night market, street food, handicrafts
- **Notes**: Only available on Sundays. Do not include on non-Sunday days.

#### Saturday Walking Street (ถนนนิมมาน, Nimman or Old City Saturday market)
- **Type**: Night market
- **Hours**: Saturday evenings only
- **Notes**: Confirm current location with web-search — market locations shift seasonally.

#### 真心市集 (JJ Market / Jing Jai Market)
- **Hours**: Weekends (Saturday–Sunday), morning
- **Type**: Organic market, local produce, crafts
- **Notes**: Date-sensitive — only Saturday/Sunday mornings. Do NOT default this into a non-weekend day.

#### 椰林市集 (Coconut Palm Market or similar)
- **Notes**: Date-sensitive — confirm operating days before including. Do not default both 真心市集 and 椰林市集 unless customer explicitly requests both.

## Hotels / Lodging Categories

> Hotel recommendations are provided as context. Chiangway does not book hotels directly unless Eric offers lodging assistance as part of the package.

### Old City Area (古城區)
- Walkable to Old City temples, night markets.
- Ideal for itineraries heavy on temple / cultural stops.
- Traffic can be slow during festival periods.

### Nimman Area (尼曼路)
- Trendy café district, walking-friendly for adults.
- Good base for shopping and evening dining.
- 20–30 min from Old City; 20–25 min from Elephant Nature Park.

### Riverside Area (濱河區)
- Quieter, scenic. Good for families wanting relaxed evenings.
- Slightly further from most tourist stops.

### Mae Rim / Nature Resort Area
- Near elephant camps, Chiang Mai Night Safari.
- Longer drive to Old City. Better for nature-focused itineraries.

### Positioning for Quote Purposes

- Lodging location affects routing and car time estimation.
- Always confirm lodging area before finalizing itinerary.
- If customer has not yet booked a hotel, note it as a missing field in the case card.

## Known Seasonal Notes (需驗證)

- **Songkran (Thai New Year, April 13–15 approx.)**: Major water festival. Roads in Old City area can be impassable for vehicles. Strongly advise customers about this.
- **Loy Krathong / Yi Peng (November)**: Beautiful festival; extremely crowded. Book early.
- **High season (Nov–Feb)**: Cool weather, ideal for families. Hotels fill up; booking lead time matters.
- **Low season / Monsoon (Jun–Oct)**: Some outdoor venues may be affected by rain. Elephant camps and outdoor markets may have limited access.
